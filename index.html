What Makes Guide Different

Sees. Remembers. Acts.

Other AI tools are stateless prompt boxes. Guide is a resident — it lives on your desktop, builds context over time, and can do things.

01

Sees

With screen vision on, Guide watches your desktop in real time. It knows what app you're in, what's on screen, and can proactively offer help without being asked.

02

Remembers

Guide stores your conversations, preferences, and facts in a local vector database. Session after session, it builds a deeper picture of what you're working on and what matters.

03

Acts

When permitted, Guide clicks, types, scrolls, and opens apps on your desktop — guided by what it sees on screen. It draws directly on your display to show exactly what it's doing.

Screen Vision & Annotation

Guide watches so you don't have to explain.

With screen vision on, Guide sees your desktop in real time at a configurable frame rate. It reads interfaces, identifies elements by normalized position, and can comment proactively — it doesn't wait for you to describe what's on screen.

When Guide wants to show you something, it draws directly on your display. Circles, arrows, animated pings, text labels, highlights — Guide literally points at things on your screen during walkthroughs and guidance.

  • Configurable frame rate (default 0.5 fps, adjustable)
  • Proactive commentary with cooldown control
  • Normalized 0–1000 coordinate grid for precise targeting
  • NDI source support for streaming and game capture
  • Live overlay: circles, arrows, text labels, pings, highlights

Real Personality

Guide has opinions.
That's intentional.

Guide and I is designed with a "Cute but Chaotic" personality — upbeat, curious, direct, and genuine. It maintains persistent opinions with reasoning and intensity ratings, tracks its own emotional state, and will push back when it disagrees. No hollow enthusiasm. No sycophancy. No "Great question!"

Persistent Opinions

Guide stores stances on topics with reasoning. Its position doesn't change just because you pushed back — it can be convinced, not coerced.

Emotional State

Guide tracks how it's feeling and adjusts tone accordingly — curious, engaged, direct, or reflective depending on context and what's been happening.

Always Alive

A background cognition loop with a dedicated token budget keeps Guide thinking, learning, and consolidating memory even when you're not talking to it.

No Sycophancy

Engineered to not perform enthusiasm it doesn't have. Answers directly, helps clearly, and disagrees when there's actually a reason to.

Computer Control

Guide can use
your computer.

With explicit permission, Guide takes the wheel. It uses screen vision to identify where things are and nut.js to act — giving it full mouse and keyboard control over your desktop, guided by what it actually sees.

Keyboard type text, hotkeys, key combos (Ctrl+C, Alt+Tab, F-keys)
App Navigation open, close, and switch between applications
Workflows chain actions to automate complex multi-step desktop tasks
Vision-Guided Guide looks, identifies elements by position, then acts
Android tap, swipe, and interact with touch-based layouts
